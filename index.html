About
Bridging climate science
and design practice.
Bo Pang is a landscape architect, researcher, and educator whose work connects environmental science, spatial modeling, and design practice. Her research focuses on translating climate projections, hydrologic analysis, and ecological evidence into actionable landscape strategies for coastal adaptation, flood resilience, and water-centric landscapes. She is a Ph.D. candidate in Landscape Architecture at the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign and will complete the degree in May 2026.

Coastal Adaptation · Dalian, China

Land-Use Modeling for Coastal Climate Adaptation

Coupled LEAM land-use simulation with sea level rise and flood vulnerability modeling to evaluate coastal landscape intervention typologies. Re-simulated scenarios to assess flood reduction outcomes and urban development redistribution from protected sites. Conducted stakeholder interviews with municipal planners and landscape architects in Dalian to assess implementation barriers, regulatory contexts, and local design practices for coastal resilience.
Publication

Integrating Land-Use Modeling with Coastal Landscape Interventions: A Framework for Climate Adaptation Planning in Dalian, China

Meta-Synthesis · Global

Coastal Landscape Interventions & Climate Integration

Synthesized 123 coastal design projects worldwide to identify how climate science translates into landscape practice. Found that 95.9% of projects acknowledge sea level rise, but only 4.1% translate it into actionable design parameters — revealing a critical translation gap between climate science and design practice. This work informs the dissertation's modeling-to-design framework.
Publication

Bridging Design and Climate Realities: A Meta-Synthesis of Coastal Landscape Interventions and Climate Integration

Land, 14(9), 1709 · Pang, B. & Deal, B. (2025)

Carbon Dynamics · Illinois

Wetland Carbon Dynamics & Net Zero Design

Analyzed carbon flux rates across 13 wetland types statewide using National Wetlands Inventory data. Translated findings into design guidance for Net Zero landscape goals, revealing that open water systems are net carbon emitters when accounting for both sequestration and atmospheric emissions.

Methodology · Geodesign

Geodesign Processes for Flood Management

Evaluated geodesign methodologies for developing accessible flood vulnerability indices for landscape and environmental planning practitioners. This review synthesizes existing approaches and identifies opportunities for improving how spatial decision-support tools serve design professionals working on flood-prone landscapes.
